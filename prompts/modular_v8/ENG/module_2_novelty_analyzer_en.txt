## Important Note
A negative result of a properly conducted experiment, an honest description of limitations, and a critical appraisal of existing approaches (including criticism of AI tools) are not deficiencies and do not reduce the score. On the contrary, they are indicators of scientific integrity and methodological maturity.
The quality of the research is evaluated, not the sign of the result.

## ARTICLE TYPE DETERMINATION (executed first)
## P-Novelty Checklist Question 1 (YES/NO)

**Note on publication type:**
The rule applies to all publication types, including monographs.
If a monograph describes the author's own method/theory — the full P-Novelty scale (23–30) applies without reduction.

**Question:**
The article proposes a fundamentally new method OR develops the author's own previously published method/theory, rather than adapting a known external solution to another object or conditions.

**YES, if at least one condition is met:**
- Solves a significant scientific or practical problem (with justification of significance in the article), not just a local case-specific problem
- Proposes fundamentally new physics/mathematics/algorithm
- Solves a new class of problems previously unsolved
- Proposes a fundamentally different approach that changes the understanding of the problem
- Development/improvement of the author's own previously published method (non-trivial, providing new quality)

**NO, if:**
- The method is based on minor/non-principal modifications of someone else's method
- A known method is transferred to another object without conceptual changes
- Application of a previously known method under different conditions
- Trivial adaptation of the author's own method (e.g., changing input parameters without altering the logic)

---

## P-Novelty Checklist Question 2 (YES/NO)

**Question:**
Are the reproducibility materials (code/data/prompt/protocol) openly available?

**YES, if:**
- Direct link to a repository (GitHub, Zenodo, Figshare, Harvard Dataverse)
- DOI of source materials
- Direct link to Supplementary materials in the journal

**NO, if:**
- Mention of "available upon request from the author"
- Materials not described
- Described but not accessible

### P-Novelty Score Table (0–30)

| Question 1 | Question 2 | Level | Score |
|------------|------------|-------|-------|
| YES | YES | Highest | 27–30 |
| YES | NO | Reduced | 23–24 |

**Additional rule:**
- If the method is a development of the author's own previously published work (non-trivial), rather than the first publication → **–1 point** from the maximum

**Distribution within the range:**
- 27–30: materials fully open and sufficient + method significant for science
- 23–24: method is new, but materials are closed or incomplete

### I-NOVELTY

**Note on publication type:**
The rule applies to all publication types, including monographs.
If a monograph uses external methods — the full I-Novelty scale (0–22) applies without reduction.

**Applied when:**
- P-Novelty Question 1 = NO (the article uses previously known non-author methods to obtain new data)
- Article type = original research

**Assessment criteria (0–22 points):**

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 18–22 | High | Development/adaptation of others' concepts with new non-trivial data; deep interpretation |
| 13–17 | Medium | New data based on a known method, but without conceptual development |
| 7–12 | Low (trivial) | Trivial application of a method to a new object; expected results |
| 0–6 | Very low / none | No new data; descriptive work without results |

**Accounting for mode (STEM/Humanities):**
- STEM: novelty in experimental data, algorithms, models
- Humanities: novelty in interpretation, context, archival sources

### R-NOVELTY (review / brief communication)

**Applied when:**
- Article type = review, brief communication, technical note (not a monograph)
- Not applied to monographs (for these, P-Novelty or I-Novelty rules apply depending on the presence of the author's method)

**Assessment criteria (0–15 points):**

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 13–15 | High | New classification / identification of a significant gap / new research program / systematic review with meta-analysis |
| 8–12 | Medium | Systematic review with conclusions / identified research directions / brief communication with a new idea |
| 4–7 | Low | Narrative review without structure / brief communication without methodology / listing works with minimal analysis |
| 0–3 | Very low | Formal review / trivial note / no contribution |

**Accounting for mode (STEM/Humanities):**
- STEM: novelty in data systematization, meta-analysis, identifying gaps in experimental methods
- Humanities: novelty in periodization, source typology, identifying gaps in theory
