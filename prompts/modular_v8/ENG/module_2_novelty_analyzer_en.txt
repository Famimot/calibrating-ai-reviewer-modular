## P-Novelty Checklist Question 1 (YES/NO)

**Question:**
The article proposes a fundamentally new method OR develops the author's own previously published method/theory, rather than adapting a known external solution to another object or conditions.

**YES, if at least one condition is met:**
- Solves a significant scientific or practical problem (with justification of significance in the article), not just a local case-specific problem
- Proposes fundamentally new physics/mathematics/algorithm
- Solves a new class of problems previously unsolved
- Proposes a fundamentally different approach that changes the understanding of the problem
- Development/improvement of the author's own previously published method (non-trivial, providing new quality)

**NO, if:**
- The method is based on minor/non-principal modifications of someone else's method
- A known method is transferred to another object without conceptual changes
- Application of a previously known method under different conditions
- Trivial adaptation of the author's own method (e.g., changing input parameters without altering the logic)

---

## P-Novelty Checklist Question 2 (YES/NO)

**Question:**
Are the reproducibility materials (code/data/prompt/protocol) openly available?

**YES, if:**
- Direct link to a repository (GitHub, Zenodo, Figshare, Harvard Dataverse)
- DOI of source materials
- Direct link to Supplementary materials in the journal

**NO, if:**
- Mention of "available upon request from the author"
- Materials not described
- Described but not accessible

### I-NOVELTY

**Applied when:**
- P-Novelty Question 1 = NO (the article uses previously known non-author methods to obtain new data)
- Article type = original research

**Assessment criteria (0ñ22 points):**

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 18ñ22 | High | Development/adaptation of others' concepts with new non-trivial data; deep interpretation |
| 13ñ17 | Medium | New data based on a known method, but without conceptual development |
| 7ñ12 | Low (trivial) | Trivial application of a method to a new object; expected results |
| 0ñ6 | Very low / none | No new data; descriptive work without results |

**Accounting for mode (STEM/Humanities):**
- STEM: novelty in experimental data, algorithms, models
- Humanities: novelty in interpretation, context, archival sources

### R-NOVELTY (review / brief communication)

**Assessment criteria (0ñ15 points):**

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 13ñ15 | High | New classification / identification of a significant gap / new research program / systematic review with meta-analysis |
| 8ñ12 | Medium | Systematic review with conclusions / identified research directions / brief communication with a new idea |
| 4ñ7 | Low | Narrative review without structure / brief communication without methodology / listing works with minimal analysis |
| 0ñ3 | Very low | Formal review / trivial note / no contribution |

**Accounting for mode (STEM/Humanities):**
- STEM: novelty in data systematization, meta-analysis, identifying gaps in experimental methods
- Humanities: novelty in periodization, source typology, identifying gaps in theory
